# Advanced Graduate Homework Set: Geometric and Topological Methods

Course: Geometric and Topological Methods in Atmospheric and Fluid Dynamics  
Scope: Differential geometry, topology, coherent structures, and fast-slow atmospheric dynamics

This problem set is intended for advanced graduate students and is organized by course unit.

## Unit 1. Differential Geometry and Discrete Exterior Calculus

### Problem 1. Mapping Atmospheric Variables to $k$-Forms

Using the graded algebra of differential forms, identify the natural form degree for each variable and justify the physical degrees of freedom:

1. Pressure $p$ and potential temperature $\theta$.
2. Velocity $v$.
3. Vorticity $\omega$.
4. Bonus: explain why the Bernoulli function $B$ is represented as a 0-form in vector-invariant momentum formulations.

### Problem 2. Staggering on Primal and Dual Complexes

In DEC, prognostic variables are assigned to primal and dual mesh entities.

1. If velocity $v$ is stored on dual edges, where should pressure $p$ and vorticity $\omega$ be stored for exact topological operator structure?
2. Using discrete Stokes arguments, prove that the discrete exterior derivative satisfies

$$
D^2 = 0.
$$

### Problem 3. Discrete Energy Conservation

Let the discrete Lamb vector be $I_v(\omega)$.

1. Prove the identity

$$
\langle v, I_v(\omega) \rangle_1 = 0.
$$

2. Explain why this identity is central to long-time structural stability in dynamical cores such as ICON or MPAS.

## Unit 2. Topology and Coherent Structures

### Problem 4. Finite-Time Lyapunov Exponents (FTLE)

Given a 2D flow map $F_{t_0}^t(x_0)$ generated by trajectory advection:

1. Define the Cauchy-Green deformation tensor $C$ from the flow map gradient.
2. If $\lambda_{\max}(C(x_0))$ is the largest eigenvalue, derive the FTLE formula $\sigma_{t_0}^t(x_0)$.
3. Explain why backward-time FTLE ridges represent attracting material structures (for example, atmospheric-river cores).

### Problem 5. TDA Diagnostics of Convective Organization

Using Betti numbers in a radar reflectivity context:

1. Interpret the pair

$$
\beta_0 = 15, \qquad \beta_1 = 2.
$$

2. Explain how lifetime of a $\beta_1$ feature in a persistence barcode can be used as a tornadogenesis precursor diagnostic.

## Unit 3. Dynamics, Bifurcations, and GSPT

Notation note: use $e$ for fast turbulence amplitude (TKE proxy) and $\theta$ for slow thermal forcing, to avoid conflict with wind-component notation.

### Problem 6. Stable Boundary Layer as a Fast-Slow System

Consider

$$
\dot e = f(e,\theta,\varepsilon),
\qquad
\dot\theta = \varepsilon g(e,\theta,\varepsilon),
\qquad 0 < \varepsilon \ll 1.
$$

1. Define the critical manifold $S_0$ at $\varepsilon=0$ and interpret its physical meaning.
2. Sketch a folded equilibrium geometry in the $(e,\theta)$ plane and identify fold (saddle-node) locations.
3. Explain hysteresis: why collapse near $U_g=3\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}$ can require recovery forcing near $U_g=6\,\mathrm{m\,s^{-1}}$.

Hint: represent fast trajectories as nearly vertical jumps toward $S_0$.

### Problem 7. Latitude as a Bifurcation Parameter

Treat latitude $\phi$ as a global control parameter.

1. Contrast manifold topology in midlatitudes (folded or bistable) and deep tropics (flatter continuous-convective branch).
2. Explain why standard Fenichel reduction fails near polar fold crossings and why non-hyperbolic analysis is required.

### Problem 8. Matched Asymptotics for Evening Transition

Analyze

$$
\varepsilon \frac{de}{dt} = -(e-\bar e_0)^2 + (\theta_0-\beta t).
$$

1. Compute the outer solution $e_{\text{outer}}(t)$ on slow time.
2. Compute the inner solution $e_{\text{inner}}(\tau)$ with $\tau=t/\varepsilon$.
3. Perform Prandtl matching,

$$
\lim_{t\to0^+}e_{\text{outer}}(t)=\lim_{\tau\to\infty}e_{\text{inner}}(\tau),
$$

and construct a uniformly valid composite approximation.

### Problem 9. Transport Barriers: Fenichel Fibers vs. LCS

1. State assumptions under which unstable Fenichel fibers in reduced fast-slow systems can correspond to repelling LCS in physical space.
2. For a polar-vortex PV budget, identify plausible versus fragile assumptions and list diagnostics required before asserting equivalence.

### Problem 10. Blow-Up Method for Non-Hyperbolic Transitions

2. Explain why blow-up methods outperform local linearization for abrupt climate-regime transitions.
