## Course Sources and Literature Guide

### Core Geometric and Dynamical Systems References

1. Arnold, V. I. *Mathematical Methods of Classical Mechanics* (Appendix 2 for geodesic-flow interpretation of ideal fluid motion).
2. Cullen, M. J. P. geometric-fluid-dynamics texts (especially *A Mathematical Theory of Large-Scale Atmosphere/Ocean Flow*) for a meteorology-oriented bridge from geometric mechanics to atmospheric approximations.
3. Holm, D. D. *Geometric Mechanics and Symmetry*.
4. Kuznetsov, Y. A. *Elements of Applied Bifurcation Theory*.
5. Majda, A. *Nonlinear Dynamics and Statistical Theories for Oceans and Atmospheres*.
6. Haller, G. (2015). Lagrangian Coherent Structures. *Annual Review of Fluid Mechanics*, 47, 137-162.
7. Brunton, S. L. and Kutz, J. N. *Data-Driven Science and Engineering* (for modern data-driven transport/regime analysis workflows).

### Specialized Articles and Technical Notes

1. Kristiansen, K. U. (2023). A review of multiple-time-scale dynamics.
2. Hek, G. (2010). Geometric singular perturbation theory in biological practice.
3. Kaper, T. J. (1999). Geometric methods for singular perturbation problems.
4. Korn, P. (2026). Exact conservation as selection principle for DEC fluid solvers.
5. Jardon-Kojakhmetov, H. and Kuehn, C. (2019). Survey on blow-up methods for fast-slow systems.
